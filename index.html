David Balbás

¡Hola! I am David Balbás, a postdoctoral researcher in Cryptography at IMDEA Software Institute in Madrid (Spain).

In December 2025, I obtained a PhD from IMDEA Software Institute and Universidad Politécnica de Madrid, supervised by Dario Fiore. Previously, I obtained a MSc in Computer Science at KTH Royal Institute of Technology (Sweden) and studied Mathematics and Physics at University of Cantabria (Spain).

During my PhD, I interned in the Applied Cryptography team at Input-Output Global and at NTT Social Informatics Laboratories. Before, I worked as a Cryptography Engineer at BERTEN.

You can find my PhD thesis here.

Interests

I work on the design and analysis of cryptographic primitives and protocols, as well as on their applications. More broadly, I am interested in theory and practice of cryptography, computer security and privacy.

My research is centered around the following topics. Don't hesitate to reach out if you are interested!

  • Succinct cryptographic primitives
  • Cryptographic proof systems and zero-knowledge proofs
  • Privacy-preserving authentication, electronic identity, and anonymous credentials
  • End-to-end encryption in real-world protocols
