David Balbás

¡Hola! I am David Balbás, a PhD student in Cryptography at IMDEA Software Institute in Madrid (Spain), advised by Dario Fiore. I am co-advised by Maribel González-Vasco and Claudio Soriente.

I joined IMDEA in October 2021. Before, I obtained a MSc in Computer Science at KTH Royal Institute of Technology (Sweden), and did my MSc Thesis at LASEC, EPFL (Switzerland). I also worked as a Cryptography Engineer at BERTEN.

Update: in autumn 2024, I am a visiting student at NTT Social Informatics Laboratories (Tokyo, Japan) hosted by Masayuki Abe.

Interests

My work focuses on the design and analysis of cryptographic primitives and protocols, as well as on their applications. More broadly, I am interested in theory and practice of cryptography, computer security, privacy and complexity theory.

I have a special interest in the following topics. Don't hesitate to reach out if you are interested!

  • Succinct cryptographic primitives
  • Verifiable computation, proof systems, and zero-knowledge proofs
  • Public-key cryptography with advanced functionalities
  • Group messaging and end-to-end encryption in real-world protocols
